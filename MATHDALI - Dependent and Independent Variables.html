DEPENDENT AND INDEPENDENT VARIABLES

A variable in Mathematics is defined as the alphabetic character that expresses a numerical value or a number.
In algebraic equations, a variable is used to represent an unknown quantity.
These variables can be any alphabets from a to z. Most commonly, ‘a’,’b’,’c’, ‘x’,’y’ and ‘z’ are used as variables in equations.
There are two types of variables that we will be tackling; the Dependent and Independent Variables.

INDEPENDENT VARIABLES
In an algebraic equation, an independent variable describes a variable whose values are independent of changes and does not rely on the other variable.
If x and y are two variables in an algebraic equation and every value of x is linked with any other value of y,
then ‘y’ value is said to be a function of x value known as an independent variable, and ‘y’ value is known as a dependent variable.

DEPENDENT VARIABLES
The dependent variable is characterized as the variable whose quality depends on the estimation of another variable in its condition.
That is, the estimation of the word variable is dependably said to be reliant on the free variable of math condition.
The variable ‘y’ is said to be a reliant variable as it depends on the other variable to get its value.

A salesman's monthly salary is determined by how many products he can sell for the whole month.
In this sentence, the independent variable is: how many products he was able to sell for the whole month.
Meanwhile, the dependent variable is: a salesman's monthly salary.
Another example is: The amount of toppings you put on your frozen yogurt at your favorite froyo shop decides the price of the frozen treat.
For this sentence, the independent variable is: the price of the frozen treat.
Meanwhile, the dependent variable is: the amount of toppings you put on your frozen yogurt at your favorite froyo shop.
Keep in mind that the independent variable is the variable that decides the outcome of the dependent variable!
